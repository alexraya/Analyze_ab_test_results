Introduction¶

This Jupyter notebook report will examine and analyze data gathered on Kaggle regarding attendance of medical appointments, and determine any possible factors that may affect patients attendance for their scheduled visits. The data is taken from over 100,000 medical appointments in Brazil, with numerous characteristics that can be examined for any correlations to attendance, including gender, appointment days, age, neighborhood, scholarship (enrolled in the Brazilian welfare program), and various medical conditions, such as hyptertension, diabetes, alcoholism, handicap, plus SMS notification. With all these factors provided, we may be able to deliver some insight into what factors may impact a patient's attendance, and what factors in combination are important to know in order to predict if a patient will show up for a scheduled appointment or not.

from pylab import rcParams
rcParams['figure.figsize'] = 12, 5

plt.subplot(1, 2, 1)
plt.ylabel("Attendances and No-Shows")
plt.xlabel("Appointment Day of Week")
plt.hist(df.query('Noshow == 0')['appointmentweekday'], color = 'green', alpha=1, bins = 5)
plt.hist(df.query('Noshow == 1')['appointmentweekday'], color = 'red', alpha=1, bins = 5)
plt.axis([0, 5, 0, 25000])

line=(df.query('Noshow == 1')['appointmentweekday'].value_counts() / 
(df.query('Noshow == 0')['appointmentweekday'].value_counts() + df.query('Noshow == 1')['appointmentweekday'].value_counts()))

plt.plot(line)

plt.subplot(1, 2, 2)
plt.title("Percentage No-Show")
plt.xlabel("Appointment Day of Week")
plt.plot(line, 'b.-')
plt.tight_layout()
plt.axis([0, 5, 0.16, 0.24])
plt.show()

As we can see from these plots, appointment numbers are higher earlier in the week and then drop off on Thursday and Friday, with no-show percentages remaining somewhat constant, and those variables alone showing lower no-show rates that the overall rate, indicating that the days of the appointment are not strong indicators of appointment no-shows.¶

Conclusions¶

An exhaustive analysis of the individual factors in the dataset uncovered some unusual correlations, particularly that age and SMS messaging together were strong indicators of whether or not someone would be a no-show an a medical appointment. These two factors combined are quite significant in strongly indicating no-shows compared to other factors in the dataset, and patients with these characteristics have outsized no-show rates compared to the overall no-show rate.¶
